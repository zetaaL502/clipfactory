- **Chrome/Edge**: Install ["Get cookies.txt LOCALLY"](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/get-cookiestxt-locally/cclelndhehlelpghfjegbekfcekjkocd)
- **Firefox**: Install ["Get cookies.txt"](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/cookies-txt/)

### 2. Sign into YouTube
- Open your browser and go to `youtube.com`
- Sign in with your Google account
- Make sure you're fully logged in (not just incognito mode)

### 3. Export the cookies
- Click the extension icon in your browser toolbar
- Click "Export as cookies.txt (Local)"
- Save the file to your computer

### 4. Verify the export is complete
The exported file should contain cookies from multiple domains, including:
- `.google.com`
- `accounts.google.com` 
- `.youtube.com`

**Important**: If your export only shows `.youtube.com` cookies and no `.google.com` entries, it's incomplete and won't work.

### 5. Upload to the app
- Open `http://localhost:5000` in your browser
- Click the Settings gear icon (top-right)
- Either:
  - Click "Choose File" and select your exported cookies.txt file
  - Or paste the entire contents into the textarea
- Click "Save Cookies"

### 6. Check validation
The app will show a green "Active" badge if cookies are saved, and warn you if the export looks incomplete.

## Why this matters

YouTube requires full Google authentication cookies (SAPISID, APISID, etc.) to bypass bot detection. Just YouTube cookies aren't enough - you need the Google account authentication cookies too.

Once you have proper cookies saved, the "Sign in to confirm you're not a bot" error should disappear completely. Let me know if you need help with any of these steps!
